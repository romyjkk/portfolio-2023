Hi, I’m Romy. I’ve been a CMD (Communication and Multimedia Design) student at the Amsterdam University of Applied Sciences since 2020. It’s a very broad study, but you can choose whatever path you want and specialise in it. I chose the front-end development path, with a bit of UX/UI on the side. While working on school projects which contain design or code, I love to be creative and making things my own way, while still following the guidelines I’ve been given. Below, you'll find a few examples showcasing my work, feel free to explore them! f

Kattenknuffelaar

HTML/CSS/JS

In my third year at CMD I followed the minor ‘Information Design’. We learned all about data visualisations and infographics. One of the projects involved creating data visualizations using code. For my project I used the D3 and the Leaflet library, providing endless possibilities for those who know what they're doing.

Amsterdam Art Center

HTML/CSS/JS

During my internship at digital agency Go2People I got to recreate several websites they’ve made in the past, including the homepage of the Amsterdam Art Center. I only got a file containing the design, I got to choose interactions (mostly hovers) myself.

Horrorflix

HTML/CSS/JS/PHP

Also during my internship, I learned a about PHP and used this knowledge to create a sign-up form. With the liberty to choose the design, I made the sign up form for ‘Horrorflix’. This is a real thing, but I didn’t look at their website and just made my own version.

The Xenomorph timeline

HTML/CSS/JS

My latest project, made in 1,5 weeks during the minor ‘Visual Interface Design’, involved creating a unique timeline for a chosen character. I chose the Xenomorph from the Alien franchise.

A-Moebe

HTML/CSS/JS

A friend of mine started his own Escaperoom. Most Escaperooms operate with a system that controls almost everything in the room, from magnets to sounds to lights and much more. I got the opportunity to work on the front-end for this system. It’s still a work in progress as it was a summer project during my non-school hours. The back-end is also under development, so there will be many more additional features.
